Master Thesis

Neural-Precomputed Scattering for Real-Time Rendering of Heterogeneous Volumes

Yudhistira Yoga Semeru, supervised by Philippe Weier M.Sc.
Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, Visual Computing MSc. program, Universität des Saarlandes (2025)

In this project, I developed a GPU-accelerated physically based path tracer using CUDA and NVIDIA OptiX for high-performance, efficient light transport simulation using Monte Carlo methods. The implemented features include Multiple Importance Sampling (MIS), homogeneous & heterogeneous volumetric rendering, and integration of neural and hardware-accelerated ray tracing. The volume is represented by Gaussian volume through an inverse rendering pipeline. The implementation of neural-based optimization improves GPU path tracing performance by 1600% and reduces volume data size by 99.6%, enabling stable 1080p global illumination path tracing at 30–60 FPS, even with dense heterogeneous volumes.

Bachelor Thesis

Mechanical Design and Control System of Ball Handling Mechanism in Mobile Soccer Robot

Yudhistira Yoga Semeru, supervised by Ir. Indrawanto, M.Eng., Ph.D
Faculty of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, Institut Teknologi Bandung (ITB) (2019)

This project focuses on the design and control system of a ball dribbling mechanism for a mobile soccer robot competing in the Indonesian Robot Soccer Contest (KRSBI-B). The dribbling system is designed to keep the ball within possession and rotationally consistent with the robot’s movement at robot speeds up to 1.5 m/s, adhering to RoboCup 2018 regulations. The control system integrates fuzzy logic, PID control, and geometric analysis to maintain natural ball rotation during gameplay.

“Hand Sign Translating Apparatus (HASTA)”

Azka Zakiyyatuddin, Yudhistira Yoga Semeru, Bambang Haryo Praditya Fathammubina, Eko Satria, Mitra Djamal, Maria Evita
Physics Contribution Seminar - Institut Teknologi Bandung (2018)

HASTA is a wearable device designed to assist speech-impaired individuals by translating hand gestures into spoken words. Using MPU6050 modules placed on each finger, the system captures hand orientation data and processes it via an Arduino Pro Micro. Detected gestures are mapped to a predefined library and converted into audible speech, helping bridge the communication gap for users who rely on sign language (BISINDO or SIBI).

“Earthquake Detection Using MPU6050 Accelerometer Sensor on an Unbalanced Mass-Based Prototype”

Daryanda Dwiammardi Djamal, Elsanita Sinaga, Laurentius Michael George, Nicola Gianina Suryadi, Yudhistira Yoga Semeru, Maria Evita, Mitra Djamal
Physics Contribution Seminar - Institut Teknologi Bandung (2018)

This project simulates earthquake oscillations using an actuator system composed of a DC motor and an unbalanced mass. The motor speed is controlled by PWM to mimic varying earthquake intensities. An MPU6050 accelerometer sensor attached to the simulation platform detects vibrations, with data processed by an Arduino to convert acceleration readings into the Modified Mercalli Scale (MMS), indicating earthquake destructiveness. The results, along with acceleration data, are displayed on an LCD for real-time monitoring.

“Development of a Wheeled Soccer Robot Platform”

Evan Febrianto, Abdullah Faqih A. M., Andi Gumarilang C. A., Ahmad Wahrudin, Azka Zakiyyatuddin, Bangsa Edwiranda, Beny Maulana A., Genesia Wahyu S., Ghovindo Surya T. S., M. Hilmi Asyrofi, M. Ihsan Hadi, M. Kamal Nadjieb, Reghina Arindadewi, Yudhistira Yoga S.
The Indonesian Symposium on Robotic Systems and Control (ISRSC) (2017)

This paper discusses the development of a wheeled soccer robot platform featuring both 3-wheel and 4-wheel locomotion configurations, dual cameras, and control systems. The robot achieves movement accuracy with a maximum error of 5%. Two cameras are utilized: a top camera for ball detection when near the robot, and a front camera for tracking the ball at greater distances.
