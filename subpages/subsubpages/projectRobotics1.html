Short Description:

The autonomous wheeled-soccer robots compete in the annual Indonesian Robot Contest (KRI) 2019.

Description:

A next generation (version 2.1) Middle size league (MSL) robot soccer developed in association with Dagozilla ITB (student robotics team) competing in the Indonesian Robotic Contest (Kontes Robot Indonesia: KRI) held annually by the Ministry of Research, Technology and Higher Education of the Republic of Indonesia. The competition is held with the Robocup Middle Sized League format.

Responsibilities:
  • Developed 3 mobile soccer robots to compete in the Indonesian Robotics Contest (KRI) 2019 for both regional and national stages.
  • Advised the team of 5 robotics mechanical engineers to build all the robots within the tight schedule.
  • Lead the mechanical development of a telepresence robot for the robotics team’s side project.
  • Manage and ensure the manufacturing batch and schedule with multiple vendors.
  • Working closely with another division (electrical and programming) to create breakthrough features.
  • The final-year project team developed the optimal ball-handling mechanism as the final-year project.
  • The general development tasks include design, manufacture, assembly, and analysis for each part of the robot.

Research Topics and Results
  • Omni-Directional Vision systems

    The development of a new version of the omnidirectional vision system. This vision system uses a Logitech WebCam C922 Pro and a Catadioptric mirror manufactured using a CNC lathe machine and chrome polishing method. This camera system is able to perceive a 6-meter radius of the environment around the robot at 360°, thus enabling full coverage of the football field. This system only costs 2% of the normal device price (€20.3 out of €1,015.3) with similar capabilities.


  • Ball-Handling system

    Performing the mechanical design and control system of a ball-handling mechanism to control the ball within the field. It enables the robot to fully control the ball in the field at a maximum speed of 1.5 m/s in any direction while maintaining the natural direction and translation of the ball to fulfill the rule requirement of the RoboCup MSL (Middle-Sized-League). The system leads to several outstanding achievements in the annual Indonesian Robotics Contest 2019.


  • Ball-kicking system

    Creating a solenoid-based ball-kicking mechanism that is able to robustly withstand the maximum kicking force of 600N while operating safely with ±60A DC current. The maximum elevation of the kicking angle is about 42.792°, enabling proper lob-kicking from every position on the field. This system is also able to set a kicking force ratio to kick from various distances into the opponent’s goalpost.


  • Omni-directional chassis systems

    Performing overall base structure modifications of the robot with a holonomic robot platform of three-Omni wheel configurations. Adding external encoders as the additional sensors for the robot’s localization while maintaining the robustness of the robot’s chassis against unavoidable collisions in gameplay.


The gameplay of the team

The team competes in the regional and national stages of the annual Indonesian Robotics Contest (KRI) 2019.
