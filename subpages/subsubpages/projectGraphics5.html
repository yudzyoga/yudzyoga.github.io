Project page: [GitHub Pages] Multi-Anchor 3D Gaussian Splatting for AD Simulator

Short Description:

A high-performance 3D Gaussian splatting framework for lightweight, high-performance, and photorealistic autonomous driving simulation. This project aims to use state-of-the-art approaches to enable hobbyists and roboticists to experiment with complex and photorealistic simulations on accessible consumer hardware.


Description:

Rendering large-scene 3D gaussian splatting environments presents a massive memory bottleneck. A typical scene containing millions of primitives easily requires hundreds of megabytes to several gigabytes of VRAM. Scaling this technology to capture the entire city blocks on consumer hardware requires strict pipeline optimization for both training and inference.

The pipeline works by separating the scene into multiple sub-segments that are centered around "Anchors" which can be loaded and unloaded dynamically from host RAM to device VRAM, ensuring flat and optimized VRAM consumption regardless of how far away the driving trajectory is. The vulkan pipeline ended up with 210 FPS at 480p, 90 FPS at 1080p, and when combined with TensorRT inference, it still produces real-time performance with 60 FPS at 480p. The graphics VRAM consumption hovers around 650 MB (including TensorRT model), demonstrating that such approach can be used on low-end consumer GPU devices. With further improvements in training and inference pipelines, it is extendable as an open-source experimentation platform for robotics hobbyists, researches, and students.
