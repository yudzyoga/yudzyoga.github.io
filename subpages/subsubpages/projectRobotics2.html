Short Description:

The autonomous wheeled-soccer robots compete in the annual Indonesian Robot Contest (KRI) 2018.

Description:

A next generation (version 2) Middle size league (MSL) robot soccer developed in association with Dagozilla ITB (student robotics team) competing in the Indonesian Robotic Contest (Kontes Robot Indonesia: KRI) held annually by the Ministry of Research, Technology and Higher Education of the Republic of Indonesia. The competition is held with the Robocup Middle Sized League format.

Responsibilities:
  • Developed 3 new mobile soccer robots to compete in the Indonesian Robotics Contest (KRI) 2018 for both regional and national stages.
  • Lead a team of 3 robotics mechanical engineers to build all the robots within a tight schedule.
  • Manage and ensure the manufacturing batch and schedule with multiple vendors.
  • Working closely with another division (electrical and programming) to create breakthrough features.
  • The general development tasks include design, manufacture, assembly, and analysis for each part of the robot.

Research Topics and Results
  • Omni-Directional Vision systems

    Performed the initial development of an omnidirectional vision system. Conducting the vision simulation using Blender3D to produce the proper catadioptric mirror. This vision system uses a Logitech WebCam C922 Pro and a Catadioptric mirror manufactured using a CNC lathe machine and chrome polishing method. This camera system is able to perceive a 4-meter radius of the environment around the robot in 360°. This system only costs 2% of the normal device price (€20.3 out of €1,015.3) with similar capabilities.


  • Ball-Handling system

    Performing the initial development of a new ball-handling mechanism through mechanical development and manufacturing. This system is able to keep the ball within possession while moving around 1.0 m/s.


  • Ball-kicking system

    Creating a solenoid-based ball-kicking mechanism that is able to robustly withstand the maximum kicking force of 600N while operating safely with ±60A DC current. The maximum elevation of the kicking angle is about 26.391°, enabling proper lob-kicking from every position on the field.


  • Omni-directional chassis systems

    Performing overall base structure modifications of the robot with a holonomic robot platform of three-Omni wheel configurations. This chassis maintains robustness against unavoidable collisions in gameplay.


The gameplay of the team

The team competes in the regional and national stages of the annual Indonesian Robotics Contest (KRI) 2018.
